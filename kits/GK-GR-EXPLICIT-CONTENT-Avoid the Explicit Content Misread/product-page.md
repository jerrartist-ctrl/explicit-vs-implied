# Avoid the “Explicit Content” Misread — Product Overview

**Last updated:** 2026-02-16

## The confusion this targets

“Explicit” often gets used as shorthand for multiple ideas:
- **Be explicit** → be clear, specific, measurable
- **Explicit content** → mature/NSFW content that may require labeling or warnings

When those meanings get mixed, people waste time, ship the wrong thing, or escalate issues that didn’t need to escalate.

## Who it’s for

- Creators, writers, editors, and managers who receive vague “be explicit” feedback
- People working with content labels, warnings, or moderation-sensitive contexts
- Anyone turning messy, ambiguous notes into a clear next action

## What this helps you achieve

- Faster alignment on what “explicit” means *in this context*
- Cleaner handoffs (briefs, edits, requirements, reviews)
- Reduced rework and fewer “misread” loops

## Common situations (high level)

- A client says “make it more explicit” and you need clarification fast
- You’re deciding whether a label/warning applies
- A team argument starts because the same word is being used differently
- You need to document what was decided so it doesn’t bounce back later
